Our History

From Plymouth Rock to the Heart of Dixie

A Vision Founded in Plymouth

The Mayflower Society was founded in Plymouth, Massachusetts, in 1897. A group of descendants recognized the urgent need for a national society to honor the memory of the 1620 Pilgrims. While the national headquarters maintains the official lineage records, the true heartbeat of the organization lives within the individual State Societies.

Bringing the Legacy to the South

The Society of Mayflower Descendants in Alabama was established on December 6, 1952, in Birmingham. We serve as the vital link between Alabama residents and their ancestral roots, providing personalized genealogical assistance and regional gatherings that keep our shared heritage vibrant.

To better serve our members across the state, we have established four distinct colony charters. These colonies allow descendants to gather locally, share research, and participate in community service.

Membership and National Connection

Membership in the Alabama Society provides a direct connection to the General Society in Plymouth. Members receive the Mayflower Quarterly and can participate in national tours and triennial meetings held in Massachusetts.
